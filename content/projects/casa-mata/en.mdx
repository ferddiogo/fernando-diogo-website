## The siting

The clearing already existed. The house simply filled the void between the trees — without felling any trunk thicker than 30 cm. The main axis was rotated 18° off north to avoid a century-old jackfruit tree.

## The program

Two wings connected by a covered walkway:

- **Private wing** — bedrooms facing the dense forest, permanent cross-ventilation
- **Social wing** — living, kitchen, veranda — opening to the clearing and sky

## The construction

Mixed structure: glue-laminated eucalyptus columns (certified) and precast concrete slabs at the dry floors. Ceramic tile roof over an exposed truss. Rainwater capture for non-potable reuse (irrigation + flushing).

## The result

320 m² across two floors. The house disappears into the forest from 80% of external views. In 5 years, vegetation is expected to grow over the rear gable — a living project.
