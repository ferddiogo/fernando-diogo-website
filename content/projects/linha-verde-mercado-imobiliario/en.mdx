## The question

The first phase of the Linha Verde BRT began operating in October 2022, connecting the South Zone to downtown São José dos Campos with 12 stations across 14.5 km. The guiding question: **how much of the city's new vertical residential production is concentrating along this corridor — and at what pace?**

## The timeline

- **2014** — First public tender for a BRT system in São José dos Campos
- **2018** — New master plan approved with development incentive laws
- **2019** — New electric BRT system approved, branded "Linha Verde"
- **2022** — Phase 1 operational (South Zone → Downtown)

## The methodology

For each station, we drew pedestrian isochrones at 5 and 15 minutes from the centroid, generating a coverage mesh. We then overlaid that mesh with vertical real estate launch lots from 2020 to 2025 — geocoded, classified by builder, typology, area, and bedroom count.

- 12 stations mapped
- 5- and 15-minute walking isochrones
- Launches cross-referenced with zoning and the post-2018 master plan

## The findings

Vertical production progressively migrated into the Linha Verde catchment:

- **2020:** 29.3% of launched units within 15 min of a future station
- **2021:** 30.6%
- **2022:** 46.6% (operations begin)
- **2023:** 9.5% (drop — likely tied to the macroeconomic cycle)
- **2024:** 38%
- **2025:** 11.2% (partial)

Most of the new stock clusters around two area bands (40–60 m² and 90–150 m²). Dominant builders: MRV, Mvituzzo, Esdras, and Reflora — together over 60% of units launched along the corridor.
